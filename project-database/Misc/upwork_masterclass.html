Workshop masterclass

Getting jobs on Upwork

A practical, step-by-step system for landing your first (and next) Upwork contract — from profile setup to submitting winning proposals.

1
Understanding the Upwork ecosystem
What you need to know before you apply for anything

Upwork is a competitive freelance marketplace. Jobs move fast — postings can receive 50+ proposals within hours of going live. Speed and consistency matter more than perfection.

Connects are the in-platform currency used to submit proposals. You must purchase connects before you can apply for jobs. Budget for this upfront — running out mid-grind will cost you momentum.

Upwork uses your IP location to signal where you are based. This can work in your favour — being perceived as EU-based (e.g. Italy) can increase your chances with European clients.

The platform has a Top Rated badge system. Achieving it can happen within 3 months if you apply aggressively and deliver well. This badge dramatically improves your conversion rate.

Mindset check: It took one experienced freelancer 50+ applications before landing their first contract. Rejection is part of the process — not a signal to stop.
2
Setting up your search pipeline
Organising your skills into searchable job categories

Create a saved list of skill-based search categories relevant to your background. Examples: Rust, C++, Machine Learning, AI, Computer Vision, Electronics, PCB Design, Robotics.

Use Upwork's search filters — "Best Matches" and "Most Recent" — to see tailored and fresh listings. Switch between both regularly throughout the day.

Enable Premium email notifications so new job alerts land in your inbox. When a notification arrives, click straight through — because you're logged in, it takes you directly to the listing.

Explore broadly — don't self-limit. Even adjacent categories (e.g. Senior Robotics Engineer, Modular Agricultural Robot) may be a strong match. Check the full job spec before dismissing any listing.

Pro tip: Organise your saved searches so you can open them in one click each morning. Treat this like a job board dashboard, not a place you browse casually.
3
Writing a winning cover letter
Using AI tools to write fast, professional proposals

Open the job listing and copy the full job description text. This is your raw input for the cover letter.

Open ChatGPT (Premium) or Claude and use a structured prompt. Paste the job description and include specific instructions about tone and formatting.

"I am applying for a job on Upwork. Here is the job description: [paste description]. Write me a tailored cover letter. Do not use em-dashes. Keep the tone professional but direct. Highlight relevant technical experience."

Review the AI output and personalise it. Add specific project references, GitHub links, or relevant experience the AI might have missed.

Always attach a relevant CV. Maintain multiple CVs tailored to different skill areas (e.g. one for embedded/electronics, one for ML/AI, one for web). Match the CV to the job before attaching.

If the job asks for portfolio links, prepare GitHub URLs or project demos that match the job domain. Have these ready — don't delay your application hunting for links.

Quality control: AI-generated text often contains em-dashes (—) and generic filler phrases. Always scan and remove these — they signal templated writing to experienced clients.
4
Pricing your proposal correctly
Milestone structuring, budgeting, and bidding strategy

Most jobs on Upwork can be structured by milestone (recommended) or by project. Milestones reduce client risk and increase your chances of being selected.

Never exceed the client's stated budget. If their budget is $250, your total bid must be ≤ $250. Going above signals you haven't read the brief.

Use AI to help break the budget into milestones. A good prompt:

"The total project budget is $250. Break this into 3 reasonable milestones with descriptions and due dates in days. Keep the milestone names professional and specific."

Aim for 3 milestones as a default. Six milestones at $30 each creates unnecessary friction for clients. A clean 3-part structure (e.g. $50 / $100 / $100) feels more professional and manageable.

Example milestone structure for a $250 project:

  • →Milestone 1 ($50): System audit and core foundation — 3 days
  • →Milestone 2 ($100): Core feature development and integration — 7 days
  • →Milestone 3 ($100): Testing, refinement, and delivery — 4 days

For project duration, always quote the minimum of any range given. If the job suggests 1–3 months, quote 1 month. Clients prefer faster timelines and it sets a productive pace.

Boosted proposals (bidding): Only use boost/bid on jobs you are highly confident about AND that are high-value. Do not routinely boost — it drains connects for marginal gain on low-probability applications.

Connects strategy: Treat connects like ad spend. Concentrate them on jobs that align tightly with your strongest skills. Spray-and-pray wastes budget.
5
The daily application routine
The habits that turn Upwork from side income to main income

Show up every single day. Upwork rewards consistency. The freelancers who succeed are those who apply morning, afternoon, and evening — not once a week.

When starting out, expect to apply for 40–60 jobs before landing your first contract. This is normal. Each rejection is data, not failure.

Open a batch of relevant job listings at the start of each session. Work through them systematically — read, evaluate, apply or skip. Don't linger on individual listings.

When a promising listing arrives via email notification, act on it within minutes. Proposals submitted early in a job's lifecycle get more attention than those submitted after 50 others.

Build a CV folder organised by skill area so you can attach the right CV instantly without back-and-forth. Label each CV clearly: cv-ml-ai.pdf, cv-embedded-electronics.pdf, cv-web-backend.pdf, etc.

If you are waiting on a collaborator or mentor for feedback and hear nothing within 3–4 hours — proceed anyway. Upwork jobs expire quickly and waiting costs real opportunities.

Key principles to carry with you
The mindset that separates earners from browsers
  • ✓Speed beats perfection. A good proposal submitted early outperforms a perfect one submitted late.
  • ✓Match every application — CV, cover letter, and portfolio links — to the specific job. Generic applications get ignored.
  • ✓Never bid above a client's stated budget. Respect the brief.
  • ✓Use AI to speed up cover letter writing, not to replace your thinking. Always personalise the output.
  • ✓Keep your milestone structure simple — 3 milestones is almost always the right number for small to mid-range projects.
  • ✓The platform rewards people who treat it like a job. If you put in the hours, the contracts follow.
  • ✓Once you achieve Top Rated status, your conversion rate increases significantly. Front-load the grind early.
